Edvard Munch

There is much debate on the mental disorders Van Gogh lived with ranging from epilepsy and bipolar disorder to borderline personality disorder and schizophrenia.

One of Van Gogh's most infamous moments was when he cut off his own year ear after an argument with his friend, Paul Gauguin. The painting on the left is a self-self portrait following the event.

With the advicement of his friends and family, Van Gogh later admitted himself to a psychiatric hospital.

Initially the structure of life at the psychatric hospital helped calm him down. He was allowed to work on his art during his stay and he often found that painting was the best remedy for his mental health. Van Gogh stated "work distracts me infinetely better tna anything else, and if I could once really throw myself into it with all my energy that might possibly be the best remedy"

However, Van Gogh's conditions re-emerged. He began to feel trapped at the spychaitric hospital and wanted to leave as soon as possible. He moved to northern France to be closer to his brother Theo. But Van Gogh continued to struggle with his mental health and he took his own life sortly after.
