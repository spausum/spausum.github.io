Mark Rothko

Rothko creates more modern paintings. His art is known for its regular shapes of different colors.


Rothko faced many hardships in his life such as anti-Semitism, poverty, and aneurysm. He also suffered from anxiety and depression. However, he used art as a form of therapy. He said, "Art is the greatest asset to mental health I have... it's still a very legitimate way of thinking and feeling and getting on with your life." He also wanted his art to touch others who were experiencing the same things as he was. He wanted his painting to communicate human emotions.


However, Rothko continued to struggle with his mental health. He eventually isolated himself and became more depressed. He committed suicide at the age of 66.


His art has become one of the most influential in the modern arts. However, a consequence of this is him being often labeled a tortured artist. However, his unique art style should not solely be accredited to his struggles with his mental health. But, his legacy should encourage creativity as an outlet and form of healing.
