Van Gogh

1853 - 1890

Vincent van Gogh battled with mental illness, including depression, anxiety, and potentially bipolar disorder

While getting treatment in a psychiatric hospital, he found art to be his best remedy.

Edvard Munch

1863 - 1944

Edvard Munch had a traumatic childhood and suffered from mental illness throughout his life.

He expressed his emotions and experiences through his paintings.

Mark Rothko

1903 - 1970

Mark Rothko was a painter who suffered from anxiety and depression, which worsened in his later years.

He used art as a way of expressing and coping with his emotions, creating abstract paintings with large-scale rectangular shapes of different colors.
