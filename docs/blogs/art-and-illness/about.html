The purpose of this website is to dispel the myth of the tortured artist and shed light on how art and creativity can serve as powerful tools for healing and self-expression. Far from being a source of suffering, art can be a way of releasing emotions, reconnecting with oneself and others, and finding meaning in one’s existence.


Art has long been associated with pain and suffering, perpetuating the notion that creativity arises solely from mental disorders. This site aims to challenge this stereotype by exploring the stories of several artists who are often labeled as ‘tortured.’ Through the analysis of their works and life trajectories, this site aims to inspire a new perspective on the artistic process - one that emphasizes its therapeutic qualities. By showing how art can help people cope with trauma, depression, anxiety, and other mental health issues, this site hopes to encourage more people to embrace their creative potential and use it for their well-being.


This site also features some resources to learn more about art therapy, a form of psychotherapy that uses art as a medium of expression and communication. Art therapy can help people explore their feelings, resolve conflicts, develop self-awareness, and enhance social skills.
